Prescreening Data

Budget and Popularity vs. Revenue

After cleaning the chosen data set, we used Tableau to provide a preliminary visualization off the CSV data alone - a "pre-story" of sorts to see whether or not the machine learning results would correlate.

One of the major things we compared was the budget vs. gross revenue - for example, whether a movie made more money the higher the budget was. The scatter plot had a loosely linear correlation, but was for the most part not very conclusive. Since we picked only the top few hundred as opposed to the entire dataset, movies that lost money were not included.

Another factor we looked at was popularity score (according to our dataset, this was affected by a number of factors, such as number of user votes, views, adding the movie to a watchlist, etc.). There was a more linear correlation between popularity and revenue (as opposed to with budget).

Last but not least, we wondered if revenue was affected by the time of the year the movie was released, and indeed there is a clear peak in June, followed by a sharp dip, and eventually peaking again in December. This makes sense, as June is usually when school is out and students have time to go watch movies (either by themselves, or with family). December is around the holidays and going to see the movies is a popular pastime for families.


Popularity vs. Vote Average, and Most Common Genres

We also examined other aspects in relation to each other - one topic of interest was whether there were any trends between the popularity score (can be extrapolated overall how many people were exposed/aware of/liked/wanted to see the film) vs. vote average (the score the user the film). There was a mild logarithmic curve to this - eventually the vote average score levels off to a certain point as opposed to being rated clearly higher (or lower) as more users voted. There are also outliers - some films are obscure but are intensely beloved by the few that voted, while other films are mainstream and marketed cleverly but only elicited a lukewarm reaction from viewers.

We also ranked the genres in order to see which were the most commonly occurring. Note: movies more often than note have multiple genres, and we wanted to see which were the most popular.

Top Grossing Movies for the Top 4 Genres

The top four most occuring genres were, respectively: drama, comedy, thriller, and action. Thus we decided to examine the top 10 grossing films within each genre. There was some overlap, as box office hit Titanic was listed under both drama and thriller. The overlap let us examine whether a movie performed relative to its competitors per genre. Interestingly enough, Forrest Gump (listed under both comedy and drama) was 10th for comedy, but missed the cutoff (it was 11th) for drama.
